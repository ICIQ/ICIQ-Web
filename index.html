  • Quantum Information

    In physics and computer science, quantum information is physical information that is held in the state of a quantum system. Quantum information is the basic entity that is studied in the burgeoning field of quantum information theory, and manipulated using the engineering techniques of quantum information processing.

  • Quantum Computing

    A quantum computer (also known as a quantum supercomputer) is a computation device that makes direct use of quantum-mechanical phenomena, such as superposition and entanglement, to perform operations on data. Quantum computers are different from digital computers based on transistors.

We Are building this open community for you to be ready to shape the future of the science and technology of Information, Computing, and artificial Intelligence in the era of Quantum Physics. This website is aiming to hosting a platform for students, researchers, scientists and engineers to catch up with the growing knowledge base and to implement quantum computers for better information processing. You are welcome to be a part of our open discussions, projects and shares related to this field.

We are mainly focusing on the following topics in this community:

  • Summarize useful theories and frameworks with rigorous scientific references crossing disciplines to form some promising roadmaps for quantum information, quantum computation and quantum intelligence development and applications;
  • Building up common facts in terms of wikipedia pages and free accessible tutorials.
  • Facilitate group studies and member interactions on classical and quantum information, computation and intelligence subjects;
  • Follow up recent breakthroughs of research in related subjects;
  • Host and organize open projects so that good ideas and discoveries can be developed into useful tools for those who can bring about more useful resources to our lives.
  • Collect and share other free resources, such as open-access computer clusters, to lower down the threshold for our community members to take part in cut-edge studies.

By making this community web open-source through GitHub, you are more than welcome to edit this webpage and generously offer inputs on associated issue tracking and wiki pages to bring in your own ideas and topics so that the future of our community will be shaped better. Don't worry about messing up our community pages. All changes can be tracked, discussed and rolled-back through GitHub's embedded functions. For more details about how this community project works, please check our wiki page.
